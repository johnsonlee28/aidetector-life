## Can You Detect ChatGPT-Written Text?

Yes. ChatGPT (including GPT-3.5, GPT-4, and GPT-4o) produces text with identifiable patterns — consistent sentence lengths, overuse of transition phrases like "furthermore" and "it is worth noting," and a distinctive lack of vocabulary variation.

Our detector analyzes these signals at the **word level**, not just paragraph level. You see exactly which words and sentences carry ChatGPT's fingerprint.

## How ChatGPT Text Differs from Human Writing

| Signal | ChatGPT Tendency | Human Tendency |
|--------|-----------------|----------------|
| Sentence length | Uniform (18-25 words) | Highly variable |
| Transition phrases | Heavy ("Moreover," "Additionally") | Sparse, informal |
| Vocabulary range | Narrow, formal | Wide, mixed registers |
| Punctuation | Comma-heavy, few dashes | Dashes, parentheses, fragments |
| Hedging | "It is important to note" | Direct statements or personal opinion |

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can you detect GPT-4o specifically?
Our detector identifies patterns common across the GPT family. GPT-4o produces slightly more natural text than GPT-3.5, but still carries detectable patterns — especially in longer passages.

### Does editing ChatGPT text avoid detection?
Light editing (fixing typos, changing a few words) usually doesn't fool detection. Significant rewriting — changing sentence structure, adding personal anecdotes, varying paragraph lengths — does reduce detection scores.
