## What Gets Rewritten

The rewrite engine targets the most common AI patterns:

| AI Pattern | Example | Rewrite Direction |
|---|---|---|
| Transition filler | "Furthermore, it is important to…" | "Also, …" / "On top of that, …" |
| Formal openers | "In conclusion, we can see that…" | "So, …" / "Bottom line: …" |
| Vague emphasis | "It is worth noting that…" | "Note that…" / "Worth mentioning: …" |
| Abstract nouns | "Has the potential to revolutionize…" | "Can change how…" |
| Passive constructions | "It can be argued that…" | "Some argue…" / "Many believe…" |
| Corporate buzzwords | "Leverage synergies to facilitate…" | Use plain verbs instead |

## This Is Not Auto-Humanizing

Rewrite suggestions are starting points, not finished replacements.

**Auto-humanizers** like Undetectable.ai swap words algorithmically to fool detectors. The output often sounds awkward, loses meaning, or introduces errors.

**Rewrite suggestions** show you a direction: "this sentence uses AI filler — here's a simpler alternative." You decide whether to use the suggestion, modify it, or take a completely different approach. The writing stays yours.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I have to use the rewrite suggestion exactly?**
No. The suggestions are starting points. Use them as a direction and edit in your own voice. The goal is writing that sounds like *you*, not writing that sounds like a rewriter trying to sound like you.

**What if I disagree with the rewrite?**
That's fine. If a sentence was flagged but you wrote it yourself and want to keep it, you can. Use the suggestion to understand *why* it was flagged, then decide whether to change it.

**How many sentences get rewrite suggestions?**
Up to 5 of the most heavily flagged sentences per analysis. Focusing on the top flagged sentences usually produces the biggest score reduction.

**Will using the suggestions guarantee a lower score?**
The suggestions target the most common AI patterns. Using them typically lowers the score. But every piece of writing is different — re-analyze after editing to verify.

**Can I use this for non-English text?**
Currently optimized for English. Non-English text may produce inaccurate results.
