## What Is AI Detector Word Highlighting?

Most AI detectors give you a single number: "73% AI." But that number tells you nothing about *where* the AI patterns are, *which sentences* sound robotic, or *which specific words* triggered the signal.

**Word highlighting changes that.** Instead of a black-box score, you get a color-coded heatmap showing exactly which words and phrases pushed your score higher — so you can fix them.

## Why Word-Level Highlighting Matters

When you know *which words* are flagged, you can:

- **Edit precisely** — target only the AI-sounding phrases, not the whole document
- **Understand patterns** — learn which words you (or your AI tool) overuse
- **Verify false positives** — see if the flagged section is actually human writing
- **Get better results faster** — fix 3 sentences instead of rewriting the whole piece

Other tools make you guess. Word highlighting shows you the answer.

## What Gets Flagged

The word highlighter detects:

- **AI filler phrases** — "it is worth noting," "furthermore," "in conclusion," "it goes without saying"
- **Formal vocabulary patterns** — words like "utilize," "facilitate," "demonstrate," "leverage"
- **Uniform sentence length** — when all sentences are similar length (a strong AI signal)
- **Low lexical diversity** — when the same words repeat in predictable patterns

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Why do some clearly human words get highlighted?**
Individual words don't trigger the flag alone — the tool looks at context and co-occurrence with other AI signals. A word highlighted in orange near several red words means the *combination* looks AI-written.

**What does the hover score mean?**
Each word gets a score from 0–100. Above 70 = strong AI signal (shown red). 40–70 = moderate signal (orange). Below 40 = clean (white).

**Can I use this to fix AI-generated content?**
Yes. Many people use the heatmap to identify which sentences to rewrite — then re-analyze to verify the score dropped. It's faster than editing blindly.

**Does word highlighting work for all AI models?**
It's trained on patterns from ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and other major models. It catches common AI writing patterns regardless of which tool generated the text.
