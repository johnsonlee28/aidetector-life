## How We Test

We use a standardized test set of 50 texts:
- 20 human-written (blog posts, essays, emails from diverse authors)
- 20 generated by ChatGPT-4o (with default settings, no system prompt)
- 10 generated by Claude 3.5 Sonnet

Each tool is tested blind on the same inputs. We measure:
- **True positive rate** — AI text correctly identified as AI
- **False positive rate** — Human text incorrectly flagged as AI
- **Speed** — Time from paste to result

All texts are between 150-500 words. We test each tool 3 times per text and use the median score to reduce variance.

## What Accuracy Actually Means

An "89% accuracy" claim sounds impressive. But what does it mean in practice?

- On **clear AI text** (unedited ChatGPT output), most tools score 85%+ correctly
- On **edited AI text** (human-revised ChatGPT drafts), accuracy drops to 50-70%
- On **formal human writing** (academic, legal, technical), false positive rates climb to 10-20%

The takeaway: **no detector is reliable enough to use as definitive proof.** Use them as screening tools, not verdict machines.
